Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to what members and newcomers ask us most. Don't see your question? Reach out and we'll help directly.

Membership

Who can join ASA-SC?
Membership is open to indigenes of Anambra State and their immediate families living in Southern California, as well as friends of the community who support our mission.
How do I become a member?
Complete the membership form linked from our homepage, or reach out to any executive member at a general meeting. New members are formally welcomed at our next gathering.
Is there a membership fee?
Yes, ASA-SC runs on member dues and contributions. Current rates are shared with prospective members during onboarding — contact us for the latest schedule.

Meetings & Events

How often does the association meet?
General membership town halls are held quarterly, with additional cultural gatherings, socials, and committee meetings throughout the year.
Can non-members attend events?
Most cultural gatherings, like our Anambra Day Gala, welcome guests and family — it's a great way to experience the community before joining.

Community Support

What kind of support does the association offer members?
Members can count on mutual aid during weddings, births, illness, and bereavement, plus mentorship and scholarship support for youth in the community.
How does ASA-SC stay connected to Anambra State?
We serve as a link between our members and the government and people of Anambra State, and support development initiatives and causes back home.

Still Have Questions?

Reach out to any member of the executive team and we'll get you the answers you need.
