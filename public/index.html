Who We Are

Founded on mutual support, built to last.

'93

Formed in April 1993 by Anambra indigenes settling in Southern California, the association began as a small circle of families looking out for one another and has grown into a lasting home for the community.

The Anambra State Association of Southern California (ASA-SC) exists to give members social and cultural support as they build their lives here, while keeping them rooted in where they come from.

We also serve as a bridge — connecting our members to the government and people of Anambra State, to the wider Nigerian community across the diaspora, and to each other, through regular gatherings, mutual aid, and shared celebration.

Whether you're newly arrived or have called Southern California home for decades, ASA-SC is a place to find community, lend a hand, and pass our traditions on to the next generation.

What We Do

Four pillars hold the association together.

01

Cultural Preservation

Keeping Igbo language, dress, music, and tradition alive for members and their children through gatherings and cultural events.

02

Community Welfare

Standing with members through weddings, births, illness, and loss — the mutual support that has anchored the association since 1993.

03

Youth & Education

Mentorship and support that helps the next generation succeed academically while staying connected to their heritage.

04

Home Connection

Serving as a link between our members and the government and people of Anambra State, and supporting development back home.
